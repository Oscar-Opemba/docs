Our case studies document security assessments we have performed across a wide range of industries and technology stacks. Each entry walks you through the problem a client brought to us, the testing we performed, the vulnerabilities we uncovered, and the measurable security improvements that followed. Every case study is drawn from a real engagement and presented in anonymized form so you can understand the depth and quality of our work without any client information being disclosed.

<Note>
  All case studies are anonymized. Client identities and sensitive details are never disclosed.
</Note>

## What each case study covers

Each entry in our case study database is structured around four areas that matter to your security and business:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Client challenge">
    The specific security problem, compliance requirement, or business risk the client needed to address before moving forward.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Testing performed">
    The exact assessment types and techniques applied — for example, web application penetration testing, API authorization review, cloud configuration audit, or multi-stage red team simulation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Key findings">
    A summary of the vulnerabilities discovered, including their severity and the attack surface they exposed. Findings reference industry-standard classifications such as the OWASP Top 10 and CVSS scores.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Business impact and risk reduction">
    The measurable outcome of remediating the findings — expressed as a percentage of critical vulnerabilities eliminated, compliance status achieved, or improvement in detection time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Case study categories

Our database spans six service types and multiple industries. Browse by the category most relevant to your environment.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Web Application Assessment" icon="globe">
    Case studies covering OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities, authentication and session management weaknesses, business logic flaws, and injection risks in customer-facing and internal web applications. Clients range from early-stage startups to enterprise platforms processing millions of transactions daily.
  </Card>
  <Card title="API Security Review" icon="code">
    Assessments of REST, GraphQL, and SOAP APIs with findings that include broken object-level authorization, excessive data exposure, missing rate limiting, and mass assignment vulnerabilities. These studies show how API flaws that appear minor in isolation can enable full account takeover or data exfiltration at scale.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cloud Configuration Audit" icon="cloud">
    AWS, Azure, and GCP infrastructure reviews covering IAM policy analysis, exposed storage buckets, unencrypted databases, insecure network configurations, and compliance gaps. Studies in this category illustrate how a single misconfiguration can expose an entire customer dataset.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Red Team Engagement" icon="user-secret">
    Multi-stage adversary simulations that test detection and response capabilities against realistic attack chains including initial access, persistence, lateral movement, and objective achievement. Results quantify gaps in mean time to detection (MTTD) and provide a roadmap for improving incident response procedures.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
