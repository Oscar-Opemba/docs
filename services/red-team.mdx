Standard penetration testing answers the question: "Do these specific vulnerabilities exist?" Red team operations answer a harder question: "What can a motivated, sophisticated adversary actually achieve against my organization — and would we know?" By simulating the full attack chain that a real threat actor would execute, red team engagements expose the gaps between your security controls on paper and your actual defensive capabilities in practice.

## What it is

Red team operations simulate advanced adversaries conducting multi-stage attacks against your organization, including initial access, persistence, lateral movement, and objective achievement. Unlike scoped penetration tests, red team engagements are goal-oriented: our team defines a realistic objective — such as accessing your financial systems, exfiltrating customer data, or achieving domain administrator access — and pursues it using the same techniques, tools, and tradecraft as real threat actors. Your blue team and SOC operate normally throughout, and their detection and response performance is assessed as part of the engagement.

## Why it matters

Red team exercises test organizational readiness against sophisticated attackers. They validate detection capabilities, response procedures, and overall security posture in a way that individual vulnerability assessments cannot. Many organizations discover through red team engagements that their detection tooling, though technically deployed, is not tuned to catch the techniques that matter most.

## Threats addressed

Red team engagements are designed to simulate the threat categories that pose the greatest risk to your organization:

- Advanced persistent threats (APT)
- Multi-stage attack chains
- Social engineering and phishing
- Insider threats
- Detection and response gaps
- Incident response procedures

<Warning>
  Red team engagements require careful scoping and explicit written authorization. Rules of engagement, out-of-scope systems, emergency stop procedures, and notification requirements must be agreed upon before the engagement begins. Contact your account manager to discuss authorization requirements.
</Warning>

## Testing approach

<Steps>
  <Step title="Threat modeling and scenario development">
    We work with your leadership to define realistic threat scenarios based on your industry, threat landscape, and strategic objectives. This shapes the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) our team will emulate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initial access simulation">
    Our team attempts to gain a foothold in your environment using the same methods real adversaries use — spear phishing, credential stuffing, public-facing application exploitation, and physical access attempts where in scope.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Persistence and lateral movement">
    Once initial access is achieved, we establish persistence mechanisms and move laterally through your environment — pivoting through systems, harvesting credentials, and escalating privileges while evading detection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Objective achievement attempts">
    We pursue the agreed engagement objectives — data access, system compromise, or privilege escalation — documenting the complete attack path and the evidence collected along the way.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Detection and response observation">
    Throughout the engagement, we record which actions triggered alerts, which were missed, and how your team responded when detections did fire. This forms the basis of the detection and response assessment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post-engagement debrief and analysis">
    At the close of the engagement, we conduct a full debrief with your red and blue teams — walking through the attack timeline, discussing detection gaps, and reviewing what worked and what did not in your response procedures.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Deliverables

You receive the following at the close of the engagement:

- Red team engagement report
- Attack timeline and methodology
- Detection and response assessment
- Recommendations for detection and response improvements
