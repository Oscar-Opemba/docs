## Why standards alignment matters to you

Choosing a testing provider that works to recognized standards has concrete, practical benefits beyond the engagement itself.

**Audit readiness.** When auditors request evidence of penetration testing, a standards-aligned report gives them what they need without back-and-forth. Our reports explicitly map findings to relevant frameworks, reducing the time your team spends translating technical results into compliance evidence.

**Defensible scope.** Standards define what a thorough test includes. If a scope decision is ever questioned — by an auditor, a regulator, or your board — alignment with OWASP, NIST, and PTES demonstrates that testing was comprehensive and professionally conducted, not arbitrary.

**Integration with your security program.** NIST framework alignment means our findings slot directly into your existing risk register. CVSS scores, business impact ratings, and remediation priorities all use language your security team already works with, reducing translation effort and accelerating remediation.

**Continuity across engagements.** Because our methodology is standards-based and repeatable, successive assessments produce comparable results. You can track changes in your risk exposure over time and demonstrate a continuous improvement trajectory to stakeholders.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Will my assessment be PCI-DSS compliant?">
    Our penetration testing engagements are scoped and conducted to satisfy PCI-DSS Requirement 11.4, which mandates penetration testing at least annually and after significant infrastructure changes. We apply both internal and external testing perspectives, cover segmentation controls where applicable, and deliver reports structured to meet QSA expectations. If you have a specific PCI-DSS scope or a QSA with particular reporting requirements, raise these during the scoping call and we will accommodate them.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do you follow OWASP testing methodology?">
    Yes. Every web application engagement follows the OWASP Testing Guide (OTG), and our test cases map directly to OWASP Top 10 categories. For API security assessments, we additionally apply the OWASP API Security Top 10 to ensure coverage of authentication flaws, broken object-level authorization, excessive data exposure, and other API-specific risks. Findings in your report are cross-referenced to the relevant OWASP category so your development team can reference the OWASP remediation guidance alongside our own.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does NIST alignment help our security program?">
    NIST SP 800-115 provides a standardized framework for planning, conducting, and reporting on security assessments. When our findings are structured to this standard, they integrate naturally into a NIST Cybersecurity Framework-based risk management program. Risk ratings use recognized CVSS scoring, remediation priorities map to NIST risk tiers, and executive summaries are written to inform the risk decisions your CISO and board need to make. This alignment also simplifies the process of demonstrating due diligence to auditors, investors, and regulators who expect NIST-based documentation.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
